**Comparative Governance & Financial Stability Analysis: Wells Fargo vs JPMorgan Chase (FY18–FY22)**

**Project Overview**

This project analyzes the financial performance and governance stability of Wells Fargo and JPMorgan Chase from FY2018 to FY2022.

The objective was to evaluate structural differences in profitability, earnings stability, operational efficiency, and credit reserve discipline. Rather than focusing on macroeconomic shocks, the study emphasizes governance quality, balance sheet strength, and financial consistency across a multi-year horizon.

The analysis was conducted using structured SQL-based financial modeling and visualized through executive-level Power BI dashboards.

**Tools & Technologies Used**

- Excel – Data consolidation and preprocessing

- MySQL – Financial ratio computation and stability analysis

- Power BI – Interactive dashboard and executive visualization

- Official 10-K Annual Reports – Primary data source

**Financial Metrics Analyzed**

 - Profitability Metrics

    - Net Income (Year-wise trend)
    
    - Return on Equity (ROE)
    
    - Return on Assets (ROA)
    
    - Efficiency Ratio (Non-interest Expense / Revenue)

- Credit & Reserve Metrics

    - Provision for Credit Losses
    
    - Allowance for Loan Losses
    
    - Provision Ratio (Provision / Total Loans)
    
    - Reserve Ratio (Allowance / Total Loans)


- Governance Stability Metrics

    - Earnings Volatility (Standard Deviation of Net Income)
    
    - Average ROE comparison
    
    - Operational Efficiency consistency
    
**Structural Financial Assessment**
      
- Earnings Stability
    
    - JPMorgan demonstrated relatively consistent profitability with controlled volatility across the study period.
    
    - Wells Fargo experienced greater fluctuations in earnings, reflecting governance challenges and restructuring efforts during earlier years.

      Insight: Earnings consistency serves as a proxy for governance discipline and operational control.

- Profitability Comparison

    - JPMorgan maintained higher and more stable ROE levels across most years.
    
    - Wells Fargo showed improvement over time but displayed greater performance variability.
    
      Insight: Higher and stable ROE reflects disciplined capital allocation and operational efficiency.

- Operational Efficiency

    - JPMorgan maintained competitive efficiency ratios, reflecting strong cost management relative to revenue generation.
    
    - Wells Fargo’s efficiency ratio exhibited higher variation, aligning with periods of operational restructuring.
    
      Insight: Efficiency stability is a key indicator of governance maturity and cost discipline.

- Credit Provisioning & Reserve Discipline

    - Provision and reserve ratios were evaluated relative to total loans.
    
    - JPMorgan maintained consistent reserve coverage aligned with its loan portfolio growth.
    
    - Wells Fargo’s reserve patterns reflected strategic adjustments during restructuring phases.
    
      Insight: Reserve discipline indicates forward-looking risk management rather than reactive provisioning.

**Key Comparative Insights**

  - Stability vs Variability
  
      - JPMorgan represents structured, stable compounding performance with consistent profitability and governance control.
      
      - Wells Fargo reflects a recovery-oriented governance adjustment phase with improving stability over time.
  
  - Profitability Structure
  
      - JPMorgan’s profitability profile demonstrates sustained capital efficiency.
      
      - Wells Fargo shows improvement but with historically higher variability.
      
  - Risk Buffer & Reserve Behavior
      
      - JPMorgan maintained steady provisioning relative to loan exposure.
      
      - Wells Fargo exhibited reserve adjustments reflecting structural governance recalibration.
  
**Final Conclusion**
  
  The analysis highlights two distinct governance profiles:
  
  - JPMorgan Chase demonstrates stable profitability, disciplined capital deployment, and consistent operational performance.
      
  - Wells Fargo reflects a governance-adjustment trajectory characterized by earnings variability followed by structural stabilization.
      
  - The comparative framework demonstrates how multi-year financial metrics can be used to evaluate governance quality, earnings stability, and risk discipline in large banking institutions.
